act justly | love mercy | walk humbly
Our Vision

To understand the causes of poverty and oppression, and to educate people in compassionate social engagement.

Our Mission

To become a training center for the younger generations in how to bring a compassionate response to social issues of our time.

We do this four ways:

  • By creating awareness
  • By educating
  • By training
  • By connecting

We create awareness by hosting dinner events whereby people are educated on issues such as:

  • Human Trafficking
  • Homelessness
  • Gangs (Prevention & Intervention
  • Illiteracy
  • Hunger
  • etc.

We educate by listening to and learning from practitioners of compassionate social justice in our community.

We train by showing people how to respond to social issues with compassion, kindness, humility and a desire to work together with churches, civic groups and other non-profit organizations in order to better our community.

We connect by providing people with ways to volunteer in areas of service within churches, civic groups and other non-profit organizations.

Our Principles

We believe in the dignity of all human beings.

We believe that churches, civic organizations, and non-profit organizations can work together in harmony and cooperation to bring justice, hope, and healing to our community.

We believe in our younger generations, and support them in their dream to better our world.

We believe that compassion, kindness, and humility are the necessary virtues that we must exhibit in order to find solutions to the poverty, hunger, and hopelessness that plague our neighbors who suffer and are caught in the cycles of poverty and despair.

Our Director

Executive Director

Our volunteer executive director is Doug Forsberg. Doug is a professor of Urban Ministry at Southern Nazarene University in Bethany, Oklahoma. Doug has over 25 years experience in compassionated urban ministry and is committed to teaching our younger generations the joy of serving others. Doug is married to Devonna Forsberg. Their only child, Cherokee, passed away in 2010 at the age of sixteen due to cancer related issues.
